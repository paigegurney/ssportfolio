My internship with the Cache County School District (CCSD) communications team gave me one of the biggest opportunities of my career. The team selected me as the web development specialist while creating the new district site, 25 school sites, and an employee portal. These sites contain anywhere from 40 to 200 individual pages, as well as hundreds of pictures, links, and buttons.

They brought me onto the team because they needed someone who knew more about web development, thus consulting me on industry best practices, such as ADA compliance, and file organization and management. Clean-up posed a challenge due to the size of the sites and the disrepair the previous website had fallen into. Nearly every image on the old pages did not have alt text, and there were many image duplicates in the backend storage.

I also took responsibility for creating, designing, and publishing various web pages. One assigned to me had fallen into complete visual disorganization, so I aimed to fix it. The resulting page became much cleaner, with clear categories and pictures to aid in readability and boost our overall accessibility rating. Near the end of my time with the team, I contributed to the design and development of the CCSD employee portal by sharing ideas about needed content.

I gained both technical and professional skills that will prove extremely valuable moving forward in my life, in any career I choose, but especially in my pursuit of Computer Science. Those skills include: how to manage large websites like the CCSD site and the individual school pages, why effective site management requires statistical tracking of user behavior, and how to connect those websites to programs to receive that information. I also experienced how to stay organized, communicate effectively, and listen intently in team meetings, and how to use different task management strategies to stay ahead of my responsibilities. A professional meeting versus a typical high school collaboration requires different social graces and skills. Adapting those professional graces through careful study of mentors around me allowed me to integrate into the team and contribute meaningfully to discussions. These habits helped me deliver well-done projects promptly without external prompting - experiences my school coursework had not fully provided. An example of this growth is when I delivered the statistical tracking data in a much shorter timeframe than I expected. My efficiency was due to my streamlining of the process by combining certain action items into one step and dedication to the task.

Most importantly, the experience gave me a real look into a career I hope to pursue - web development. This experience offered a snapshot into what my day-to-day life might look like, how much collaboration goes into website creation, and the different moving parts needed to get the job done. It confirmed my desire to pursue a career in full-stack web development for at least part of my life.
